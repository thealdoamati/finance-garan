Luiz Cezar Fernandes

Founder

Luiz Cezar Fernandes is an undeniable name in the Brazilian financial market. Founder of Banco Garantia in 1971 and Banco Pactual in 1983, he revolutionized the sector with a culture of meritocracy and innovation, establishing Pactual as the largest investment bank in Latin America.

Furthermore, he played a key role in creating essential market structures such as CDI, SELIC, and CETIP, which today support the Brazilian financial system. Fernandes also founded Brazil's first asset management company at Banco Pactual, a market that now comprises over 800 firms. He led major restructuring and privatization operations, including Tupy, Lacta, Horita, Varig, and Portugal Telecom, as well as pioneering the entry of foreign investors into the country. He was also at the forefront of significant negotiations, acting as a consultant in Brazil’s external debt negotiation and various mergers and acquisitions that shaped the national market.
